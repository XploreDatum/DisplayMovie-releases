— 01

Four moving parts. One quiet film.

  1. 01

    Capture

    Every 1 second to 3 hours, DisplayMovie takes a silent screenshot via Apple's ScreenCaptureKit. Multi-display? It composites them preserving your screen arrangement.

    captureIntervalSeconds: 300
  2. 02

    Dedupe

    Each frame is SHA-256 hashed against the previous. Pixel-identical frames are discarded. An untouched screen never makes it into the film — and never costs you disk space.

    SHA256 ≡ skip
  3. 03

    Compile

    At local midnight, on quit, on sleep, or at a custom time, the day's unique frames stitch into a single MP4 via AVFoundation. H.264 / HEVC, 1–60 fps, native / 4K / 1080p / 720p.

    AVAssetWriter → mp4
  4. 04

    Share

    OCR-search any visible text across the entire timeline. Build a movie from any date range. Auto-upload to YouTube with the title format and visibility of your choice.

    Vision · FTS5 · YouTube API

Built for actual use.

1 second → 3 hours

Range compiler

Build a movie from any date range with any sampling cadence. Last week. Last month. Just Tuesdays.

Lifecycle aware

Pauses on screen-lock, on system sleep, and below your battery threshold. Resumes automatically. Optional launch-at-login via SMAppService.

— 04

Your screen never leaves your Mac.

No telemetry. No analytics. No "anonymous diagnostics". OCR runs locally via Apple Vision. The metadata DB lives in your storage folder. Movies live where you put them. The only network call DisplayMovie ever makes is to YouTube — and only if you explicitly connect your account and turn on upload.

— 06

Quick answers.

Does it slow my Mac down?

Captures take a few hundred milliseconds via ScreenCaptureKit and happen on your chosen interval. The default 5-minute cadence is undetectable in practice. The dedupe SHA-256 over a Retina frame is < 100 ms on Apple Silicon.

How much disk space will it use?

Each Retina PNG ranges 1–5 MB depending on content. At 5-min intervals over an 8-hour day, expect ~300–500 MB before dedupe. With Keep Screenshots After Compile off, the day's PNGs are deleted once the MP4 is built.

Why two permission prompts on first launch?

macOS gates screen recording twice: a coarse "Screen Recording" permission in System Settings, plus a per-app dialog asking to "bypass the system picker". The wizard surfaces both inline so it's not a surprise later.

Can I upload to anything other than YouTube?

Not in v1. The architecture (`Sharing/YouTubeService.swift`) is generic enough that adding Vimeo or self-hosted upload is a single new file.

What about Intel Macs?

Currently Apple Silicon only. The codebase compiles fine for x86_64 but ScreenCaptureKit's performance characteristics differ enough on Intel that we don't want to ship a build we haven't tuned.

Is it open source?

The release artifacts and this site are public at XploreDatum/DisplayMovie-releases. The application source is currently kept private while we polish v1.
